Frequently Asked Questions

Loss can be bewildering. We've done our best to clarify the details and give you everything you need to get started.

If you still have questions, our team will be happy to help. Please reach out at support@empathy.com. If you're already using the app, you can also ask a Care Manager.

Who is Empathy for?

Empathy is a benefit for bereaved families, offering personalized, step-by-step guidance through the emotional and practical challenges of losing a loved one. This benefit is provided by employers, insurance carriers, or other partner organizations.

What does Empathy help with?

Empathy covers all of the many aspects of loss: each task, decision, and challenge you may be facing, and all of the information and advice you need to be confident that you are progressing correctly. Whatever you need assistance with—planning a funeral, applying for benefits, settling your loved one’s estate, contending with family tensions, dealing with overwhelming grief, and much more—our expert guidance and innovative tools can help.

What services are available through Empathy?

An Empathy account gives you access to the Empathy app, a comprehensive platform that provides guidance and assistance for the various challenges that follow the loss of a loved one. Families can connect with a Care Manager through a live chat, where they will learn about you and your family's situation. They can provide you with compassionate guidance, help you with certain tasks, and connect you to professional service providers in your area if you need them.

How does the Empathy app work?

After asking a few introductory questions, the app presents a personalized experience that helps you navigate the challenges of loss.

Offering immediate guidance, you can access your personal Care Plan, a step-by-step roadmap containing all the tasks you and your family should tackle, laid out to best reflect your needs. Tap on each step to get all the information and guidance you need to complete it, and for links to tools within the app that can assist you with specific steps.

From each step, you can also access guides that give you further, in-depth information about the subject. You can check off steps that are completed or hide them from the timeline, set yourself reminders to complete particular steps, and assign them to other family members using the app's collaboration features.

You can also filter your Care Plan to show only the steps related to a particular topic. The topics in the app are:


  • Immediate Arrangements
  • Informing Others
  • The Funeral
  • Searching for Documents
  • The Will
  • Identity Theft Prevention
  • The House
  • Probate
  • Applying for Benefits
  • Managing Accounts
  • Bills & Debt
  • Property & Assets
  • Personal Belongings
  • Taxes

In addition, the app contains extensive resources for helping to navigate grief, including a daily healing practice that helps build and maintain a routine, audio episodes, guided meditations, and a space for reflecting on your own emotions.

What tasks can Empathy do for me?

Empathy gives you access to innovative tools that can lessen the burden for you. For example, the Obituary Creator can craft a meaningful tribute honoring your loved one's life based on your answers to a few questions. The app also allows you to upload and organize all important documents in your secure digital vault. With our Funeral Homes tool you can easily locate top-rated funeral homes in your area and receive price quotes based on your specific requests.

Your Care Manager can also help you with a range of tasks, including obtaining necessary forms, contacting relevant authorities and offices, locating service providers, scheduling calls and visits, finding professionals in your area, and more.

Who are the Empathy Care Managers?

Our in-house team of Care Managers is composed of experienced professionals who come from diverse backgrounds in social work, behavioral health, and other relevant fields. Each member of our team undergoes comprehensive training in grief support under the direction of David Kessler, the foremost expert on grief and loss. Additionally, the Care Team receives instruction in estate planning and probate to provide tailored guidance to assist families in navigating loss, even in the most challenging situations.

While your Care Manager can assist with general guidance and information about legal and financial matters, they are not legal or accounting professionals, and cannot provide you with specific legal or financial advice. (Please see our Terms of Service for more details.)

If you need specific legal or financial advice, a Care Manager is available to help you search for local professionals who can help you and your family.
